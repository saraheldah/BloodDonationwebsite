Importance of the Blood Supply



Every two seconds of every day,people around the world need blood.
It is essential for surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and
traumatic injuries. Whether a patient receives whole blood, red cells,
platelets or plasma, this lifesaving care starts with one person making
a generous donation.


Facts About Blood Needs
  1. Less than 38 percent of the population is eligible to give blood or platelets.
  2. The average red blood cell transfusion is approximately 3 units.
  3. A single car accident victim can require as many as 100 units of blood.
  4. Blood and platelets cannot be manufactured; they can only come from volunteer donors.
  5. The blood type most often requested by hospitals is type O.
  6. One donation can potentially save up to three lives.
  7. People diagnosed with cancer need blood, sometimes daily, during their chemotherapy treatment.

Blood Supply Statistics
  1. About 118.4 million blood donations are collected worldwide. 40% of these are collected in high-income countries,
    home to 16 % of the world’s population.
  2. 62 countries report collecting fewer than 10 donations per 1000 people. Of these, 34 countries are in the WHO
    African Region, four in the WHO Region of the Americas, six in the WHO Eastern Mediterranean region,
    three in the WHO European Region, six in the WHO South-Eastern Asia Region, and nine in the WHO
    Western Pacific Region. All are low- or middle-income countries.
